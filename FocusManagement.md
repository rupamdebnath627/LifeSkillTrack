# Focus Management


## 1. What is Deep Work

#### What is Deep Work?
**Deep work** is a set of professional activities performed in a state of distraction free concentration that pushes your cognitive abilities to the limit. These efforts create new values and improve your skill and are hard for others to replicate.

## 4. Summary of Deep Work Book

#### According to author how to do deep work properly?
Here are the key points to deep work properly:
- **Adopt a Deep Work Philosophy:** The first step is adopt a strategy for deep work. These strategies include monastic, bimodal, rhythmic and journalistic. This will determine whether you wish to go for all or nothing approach or do it free time.
- **Ritualize and Time-Block Your Sessions:** In this step you need to decide in advance and set a routine, and also you need to block some time and set a duration.
- **Eliminate Digital Distractions:** In this step you need to find a way to prevent getting distracted by your phone and notifications. One good solution would be to schedule some breaks and only in those break you check your phone.
- **Execute Like a Business:** This step involves on focusing wildly important topics first. Also, you need to keep a track of what you are doing and embrace the boredom that comes with deep work. 

#### How can you implement the principles in your day-to-day life?
I can implement these principles in the following ways:
- The first step I will take is to block and fix a time whenever I am trying to learn some new tech stack.
- Second, would be to create a schedule and practice it daily, and turn it into a habit.
- Third, I will do everything to avoid distraction like putting phone in silent, going in place with calm environment, etc.
- Finally, I will to embrace boredom and try to continue with the work even if it feels monotonous. 

## 5. Dangers of Social Media

#### What are the dangers of social media, in brief?
Some of the dangers of the social media are:
- **Loss of attention and focus:** Constant app notification and update and the urge to check your feed too often reduces you ability to focus on anything.
- **Prone to cyberbullying and online harassment:** Becoming a social media addict and the habit to get involved in every fruitless topic or urge clicking on anything that looks fancy will eventually increase your chances of facing these things.
- **Seeking fake validation:** The endless habit of posting every incident or occasion of your life will lead you to seek online validation, which in turn will also lead to a false sense of achievement.
